The Long Winter of ‘69 was a major political dispute and constitutional crisis in the United States that prevented the election of the president in the immediate aftermath of the 1968 presidential election.

In 1968, neither Republican candidate George Romney nor Democratic candidate Hubert Humphrey succeeded in attaining a majority of votes in the Electoral College due to the actions of American Independent Party candidate George Wallace.

Sources:

David Nevin - Muskie of Maine

Not a very useful source, since it was written before Muskie dropped out of the primaries, but a good source on his personality nonetheless.

Bates College - Muskie Archives

A great host of both primary and secondary sources to Muskie’s 1972 campaign and his larger political career, one of the main things I used in order to mine Muskie quotes and engage with his positions (or lack of positions). Every useful Muskie speech I found I found somewhere in the Bates College Archives.

Senate Watergate Commtitee - Hearings, Reports and Prints of the Senate Select Committee on Presidential Campaign Activities

The most comprehensive report on the Nixon administration’s campaign against Muskie, along with all the other potential Democratic candidates of the year. Much of what I used was from this.

CREATIVE LIBERTIES

The specific facts about Muskie’s fundraising (he indeed received funds from Republicans) such as receiving money from SPACE or ADEPT (milk industry lobbying organizations) were, in all likelihood, fabricated by Stewart Mott, a supporter of George McGovern in the primaries. They were included in the mod as a creative decision, and I have found no other evidence for whether or not Muskie actually did receive funding from these donors.

For gameplay reasons, I have made the creative choice to make the scenario winnable easily - play on Hard for a more realistic experience.

Other Sources

Much of what I used for the running mates were speeches by the running mates themselves, or statements made by people close to them. One of the only sources I did use for the running mates was this:

David T. Ballantyne - New Politics in the Old South: Ernest F. Hollings in the Civil Rights Era

This book was readily available for me and provided much information on Hollings’s positions.
